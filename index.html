NeverTLS

About

This website is a lot like neverssl.com, but with the proper protocol name. It provides an intentionally insecure website for you to visit when you need to allow someone to hijack your traffic for the purposes of logging into the local wifi. Simply type "http://nevertls.com" in your URL bar and you'll be redirected to the local captive portal login screen.

Under the Hood

nevertls.com does not use TLS (the precursor to which was known as SSL) to serve your connection. In other words, it uses the old, insecure HTTP protocol instead of the newer, secure HTTPS protocol. Normally this is a very bad idea (and hopefully your browser is getting better and warning you about the insecure nature of this website).

Unfortunately, some networks use a form of authentication that requires them to hijack your connection for the purpose of redirecting you to their local login page (often called a "captive portal"). TLS/HTTPS rightfully prevents this from happening. As the web continues to move toward using TLS/HTTPS everywhere, it becomes more difficult to connect to these kinds of networks. Thus, having an intentionally insecure page such as this one (or neverssl.com) that you can visit when you need to connect to such a network is useful, at least until networks move to better forms of authentication.
